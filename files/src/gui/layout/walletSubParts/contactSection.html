About this contact's Info
Balance is the net of every completed transaction with this contact in the selected currency. Positive means they owe you; negative means you owe them.

Your / Their Available Credit show how much either of you can still spend with the other before bumping into the credit limit. These update on every transaction plus a periodic background refresh.

Their Credit Limit is the maximum you're willing to let this contact owe you — set it from the Settings tab of this modal. Their Relay Fee is the percentage this contact charges you when routing your transactions through their node in a multi-hop payment.

About Transactions
Every money-movement between your node and this contact, in reverse chronological order — sent, received, and any relayed transactions that hopped through either side. The status icon on the left shows whether a transaction is pending, in-flight, completed, or failed; click any row to open the full transaction-details modal (txid, signatures, previous-txid chain link, and so on).

Only the last transactions load by default to keep the modal snappy. Refresh pulls the latest from the database — helpful right after you've sent or received, or after a chain sync finished in the background.

About the Payback tab
This tab lists the ways this contact will accept settlement of debts you owe them — bank account, PayPal, Bitcoin address, and so on. The list is fetched live from their node every time you open the tab; nothing is cached or stored on this device, so the information is always as fresh as what they're currently sharing.

Tap any row to see the full details with copy-to-clipboard buttons for each field. Use the currency selector to filter to a specific currency when the contact shares methods in more than one — changing the filter refetches automatically. To pull a completely fresh copy at any time, close and reopen this tab.

If the tab says the contact is not sharing anything, it means either they haven't added any payback methods yet or they've chosen not to share them with you — each method has a share policy (share automatically or never share).

Opening this tab fetches methods live from the contact. This might take a moment…

About Status
Online means your wallet successfully pinged theirs within the last few minutes. Offline usually just means their node isn't reachable right now (asleep, offline, Tor circuit issue) — it does not mean the contact is deleted or blocked, just unreachable.

Chain reports the integrity of your shared transaction history. A checkmark means your two chains agree — every transaction your side has links correctly to theirs. A warning means there's a gap or mismatch that needs to be reconciled.

Check Status forces a fresh ping and a chain sanity check. If the chain shows a gap, try this first — it often clears up stale state from a dropped connection. If the gap persists, the contact is out of sync and the chain-drop workflow below guides you through resolving it.

About Settings
Name is how this contact appears in your wallet — only you see it, changing it doesn't notify the other side.

Credit limit is the maximum amount you're willing to let this contact owe you in the selected currency. Lowering it below what they currently owe is allowed — it just means they can't accrue more until they've paid back below the new ceiling.

Fee is the percentage you charge them when routing their multi-hop transactions through your node. Keep it small or zero for trusted contacts; raise it if you're a busy relay node.

Block stops all new transactions with this contact and hides them from your contact list. Existing transactions are not touched. Delete removes the contact entirely — existing transactions stay on both chains, but you can't make new ones without re-adding them as a contact first.

Pending Contact Requests

About Pending Contact Requests
Incoming requests from other wallets that want to connect with you. Accepting creates a bilateral contact record (and adds any currencies they use if they aren't already in your Allowed Currencies list). Rejecting silently drops the request — the other side isn't told. Outgoing requests you've sent that are still waiting on the other party also live here until they respond.

Subnote on currencies. If a request carries a currency outside your Allowed Currencies list, you'll see an explicit per-currency accept toggle so you can pick which to allow rather than accepting everything blindly. The default behaviour (accept what the contact offers) can be changed under Settings → Feature Toggles → Contacts → Auto-reject Unknown Currency.

How to handle this request
Their addresses — the three pills up top (Tor, HTTPS, HTTP) are how your wallet reaches theirs. Click a pill to view the full address. Whichever of the three you've configured your wallet to prefer (Settings → Network → Default Transport Mode) is the one that'll be used for outgoing traffic.

Contact name — a local-only nickname for your wallet. The other side never sees it. Rename them anytime from the contact detail modal.

Each currency is a separate line of credit. Click the row to set the Fee % (what you charge to relay their transactions in that currency) and Credit Limit (the maximum you're willing to let them owe you in that currency). If you don't touch them, your wallet defaults apply (Settings → Payments → Default Fee and Default Credit Limit). A currency marked new is not in your Allowed Currencies yet — accepting will add it.

Accept All takes every currency at once with whatever fee + credit you've set (or the defaults). If any are still at defaults you'll get a confirmation first. Delete Request silently drops the request — the other side isn't told. Block Contact prevents future requests from them.
