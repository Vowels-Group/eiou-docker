Payback Methods

How payback methods work
Payback methods are how you prefer to be paid back — a bank wire, a Bitcoin address, Pix, PayPal, Venmo, Wise, and more.

Every method is encrypted at rest on this node (per-row, AES-256-GCM keyed to the wallet) before it hits the database, whatever the type. How much is hidden in this list depends on the rail.

Bank wire and similar typed rails show only the last 4 of the IBAN or account number (•••• + last 4) and redact everything before it; the full value is released only after you unlock with your auth code.

Custom is free-text you write yourself — the list shows the first 80 characters as a preview since the whole point of the field is to be descriptive. Treat it like a note, not a secret: anything after that 80-char cut-off is hidden until unlock, but don't paste bank details into it and expect the same redaction as a typed rail.

Each method also carries a share policy so you control whether a contact asking how to settle up receives it automatically or never sees it at all.
