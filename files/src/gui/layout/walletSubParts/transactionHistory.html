About Recent Transactions
Your most recent transactions across every contact and routing hop — direct sends, P2P relays, contact-request transactions, and pending items are all shown here. Search by counterparty name, address, or description; filter by direction, type, or status; click any row to open the full transaction detail.

Search database. The search bar above filters the rows currently loaded on this page. If the transaction you’re looking for is older than the currently visible window, click Search database (or press Enter) to run a server-side search across your entire transaction history — it respects the direction / type / status filters you’ve selected, replaces the table with matches (capped at 500), and adds a Clear search link to return to the default view.

Subnote on counterparty names. Unknown means the other side isn’t in your contacts anymore — typically a P2P send, a relay hop, or a contact you’ve since deleted. RestoredContact<N> names are auto-assigned when a contact reappears after deletion or when this node is re-seeded — rename them from the Contacts tab.

Subnote on “—” in the Amount column. Rows showing an em-dash instead of a number are contact-request transactions — the first transaction exchanged to establish a bilateral relationship. They carry no money (the amount is zero by construction), which is why the amount is suppressed. The Type column flags them as “Contact” on desktop; on narrow viewports that column is hidden, so the em-dash is replaced with a Contact request label instead.
